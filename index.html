1. Bears Have an Incredible Sense of Smell

Bears have one of the best senses of smell in the animal kingdom. A polar bear, for example, can detect a seal's scent from up to 20 miles (32 kilometers) away, even if the seal is buried under 3 feet (1 meter) of snow! This powerful sense of smell helps them locate food, mates, and avoid danger.

2. Bears Are Surprisingly Fast

Despite their large size, bears are incredibly fast runners. A grizzly bear can reach speeds of up to 35 mph (56 km/h)—faster than Usain Bolt, the world's fastest human!Black bears are also speedy, clocking in at around 30 mph (48 km/h).

4. Bears Hibernate, But It’s Not True Sleep

During hibernation, a bear's heart rate drops from about 40-50 beats per minute to just 8-10 beats per minute. Unlike other hibernating animals, bears do not eat, drink, urinate, or defecate during this period, which can last up to 7 months. Female bears even give birth and nurse their cubs while hibernating!
